# VSL SCRIPT — ACQUISITION MEDIA
### Word-for-Word Script for Anas Agha

**Format:** Talking head, face to camera, no voiceover, no green screen
**Target duration:** 2.5-3 minutes at natural pace
**Tone:** Direct, intelligent, slightly irreverent — founder energy, not pitch energy
**Setting:** Clean, minimal background. Dark wall or subtle office. Nothing distracting.

---

## PRE-SHOOT NOTES

Read this aloud three times before recording. The rhythm matters as much as the words. Natural pauses are marked with [pause]. Emphasis is marked in CAPS. Do not rush — the slower you speak, the more confidence it projects.

Do not read this word for word robotically. Know it well enough that it sounds like you're saying it, not reciting it.

## POST-SHOOT NOTES

**Pacing**: The whole script runs approximately 2 minutes 50 seconds at a natural speaking pace. Do not rush. Pauses are part of the delivery — they signal confidence, not uncertainty.

**Eye contact**: Stay on the lens. Not the screen, not the monitor — the lens. The moment your eyes drift, you lose trust.

**Energy management by segment**:
- Segments 1-2 (Hook + Agitate): Calm and direct. Almost cold. You're stating facts, not performing outrage.
- Segments 3-4 (Credibility + Mechanism): Warm but focused. You're sharing something you know deeply.
- Segment 5 (Proof): Slightly more conversational. You're telling a story.
- Segments 6-7 (Offer + Guarantees): Clear and confident. No hedging.
- Segment 8 (CTA): Completely matter-of-fact. The scarcity is real — let it be real.

**What to avoid**:
- Don't smile too early — it undercuts the hook
- Don't look down at notes — know this before you record
- Don't add filler sounds (um, uh, like) — they undermine the authority the script is building
- Don't rush the proof section — the specific numbers are the most persuasive thing in the video

**Technical**:
- Record at least 5-6 full takes. The best take is usually take 4 or 5 when you've loosened up but haven't over-rehearsed
- Record a version at normal pace and one slightly faster — compare both before editing
- Leave 2 seconds of silence at the top and bottom of each take for editing room

**Thumbnail / first frame**: Consider a first-frame text overlay: "Your agency doesn't know your CPL." — captures attention in feed before play.
